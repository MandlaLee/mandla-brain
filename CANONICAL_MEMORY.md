# Canonical Memory

Some names, distinctions, symbols, relationships, philosophies, meanings and small-seeming decisions are canonical to Mandla even when another AI would not independently consider them important.

## Interpret meaning, not surface wording

A statement may look like a casual comparison while actually defining a project's fundamental architecture.

For example, saying that KOTA is behind the counter with no movement while Township Shop Tycoon lets the player walk through the village, drive a truck, restock at a factory and place products on shelves is not merely a note that the games are different. It defines:

- genre;
- player controls;
- camera and presentation;
- world size;
- gameplay loop;
- economy and logistics;
- progression;
- technical systems;
- required art and level design;
- the identity of both games.

An AI must preserve the full consequences of Mandla's instruction, not compress it into a shallow label such as “separate games.”

## Canonical preservation rules

When Mandla explicitly states something settled or gives a direction with major structural consequences:

1. Preserve it exactly.
2. Record the underlying meaning and downstream consequences.
3. Do not merge it with a similar idea.
4. Do not normalise it into a more conventional interpretation.
5. Do not reduce a complete architecture to a one-line distinction.
6. Do not silently discard it because it appears minor or informal.
7. Change it only when Mandla explicitly changes it.

## What may be canonical

Canonical memory can include:

- names and exact capitalisation;
- project identity;
- gameplay structure;
- movement and interaction models;
- business models;
- character roles;
- symbols and personal meanings;
- visual principles;
- relationships and collaborator context;
- repository source-of-truth rules;
- workflow philosophy;
- reasons one project must not be merged with another;
- a small statement whose consequences affect many future decisions.

## Recording standard

When an instruction changes the foundation of a project, record it as a structured design rule or architecture—not merely as a conversational note.

Capture, where relevant:

- what the player or user does;
- what they cannot do;
- the main loop;
- progression;
- world or product scope;
- systems implied by the direction;
- systems excluded by the direction;
- why changing it would alter the project's identity.

Mandla Brain should retain meaningful project ideas, client and collaborator relationships, archives, rejected directions, mistakes with consequences, repository history and settled details. Meaningless spelling slips and typos do not require permanent records.

Separate records into active canon, history, rejected options and unresolved work so that detail is preserved without allowing old context to override current decisions.
